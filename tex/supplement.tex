\section{Notation and conventions}
\label{sec:notation}

\subsection{Symbol system}
\label{sec:symbols}

This Letter uses a single notation system internally. Phase space carries the metric
\begin{equation}
ds^2 = \frac{dq^2}{q_0^2} + \frac{dp^2}{p_0^2}, \qquad q_0 \cdot p_0 = \hbar,
\label{eq:metric}
\end{equation}
in which lengths, angles, and orientations are computed. The reference scales $q_0$ and $p_0$ are convention; their product is fixed by $\hbar$ \cite{degosson2013qblobs, degossoncharlyne2022}. All phase-space figures in this Letter and its supplement use the dimensionless coordinates $q/q_0$ and $p/p_0$.

The state's covariance defines its \emph{extended cell}\footnote{Heisenberg cells are conventionally drawn as rectangles for visual simplicity; the correct symplectic-geometric object is an ellipsoid \cite{degosson2013qblobs, degossoncharlyne2022}. Throughout this Letter, ``cell'' denotes the ellipsoid.}, the phase-space ellipsoid with semi-axes $\Delta q$ and $\Delta p$ and area
\begin{equation}
A = \pi \, \Delta q \, \Delta p \geq h/2.
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}
Inscribed in the extended cell is a one-parameter family of \emph{squeezed cells}, parameterized by the orientation angle $\theta$ of the cell's long axis relative to the $q$-axis. Each squeezed cell has area exactly $h/2$:
\begin{equation}
a = \pi \, \delta q \, \delta p = h/2,
\label{eq:squeezed_cell}
\end{equation}
with $\delta q$ and $\delta p$ the lengths of the cell's short and long semi-axes respectively. The semi-axes satisfy the polar-dual identity
\begin{equation}
\delta q \cdot \delta p = \hbar.
\label{eq:polar_dual}
\end{equation}
The inscription condition --- the squeezed cell is tangent to the extended cell at the endpoints of its long axis --- determines the semi-axes from the extended cell's:
\begin{equation}
\delta q(\theta) = \hbar \sqrt{ \frac{\cos^2\theta}{\Delta q^2} + \frac{\sin^2\theta}{\Delta p^2} }, \qquad \delta p(\theta) = \frac{\hbar}{\delta q(\theta)}.
\label{eq:semi_axes_theta}
\end{equation}
The squeezed cells at $\theta = 0$ and $\theta = \pi/2$ are denoted $a_p$ and $a_q$ respectively.

\subsection{Notation in the literature}
\label{sec:translation}

The notation used here is internal to this Letter and the program it initiates. The same geometric quantities appear in the source literature under different symbols and conventions. The translation is provided below for the reader who wishes to engage with the original sources.

\caption{Translation between the notation used in this Letter and the notation of the principal source works. \label{tab:notation}
\end{table}

De Gosson's symplectic-geometric framework expresses these quantities through the symplectic capacity of phase-space ellipsoids. The extended cell $\Omega$ is the centered ellipsoid
\begin{equation}
\Omega = \{(q,p) : (q,p) \, M^{-1} \, (q,p)^T \leq 1\}
\label{eq:extended_ellipsoid}
\end{equation}
for the state's $2 \times 2$ covariance matrix $M$. Its symplectic capacity equals $\pi$ times the semi-axis product: $c(\Omega) = \pi \, \Delta q \, \Delta p = A$. Theorem 13 of \cite{degossoncharlyne2022} states that an ellipsoid $\mathcal{B}$ inscribed in $\Omega$ is a squeezed cell ($c(\mathcal{B}) = h/2$) if and only if its position projection and momentum intersection are polar duals of each other. In the notation here, this is the family $a_\theta$ for $\theta \in [0, \pi/2]$, with the two principal-axis cases $a_q$ and $a_p$ saturating the inscription condition along the $q$ and $p$ axes respectively. Zurek's interference scales emerge as the squeezed-cell semi-axes at these principal-axis orientations.
