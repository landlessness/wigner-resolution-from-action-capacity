\title{Wigner Resolution from Action Capacity}

\author{Brian S. Mulloy\orcidlink{0000-0002-1803-3172}}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher, Corktown, Detroit, Michigan, USA}
\email{bmulloy@umich.edu}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
Smoothing the Wigner function gives a non-negative phase-space portrait of a quantum state, and the smoothing scale has been freely chosen. We show that the scale is fixed by the state. The state's covariance defines an elliptical Heisenberg cell of action capacity $\pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p$, admitting a family of de Gosson quantum blobs deforming at constant action $h/2$. Convolving the Wigner function to a single $h/2$ blob renders it non-negative. Integrating over the family reaches the resolution $\pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$, the symplectic polar dual of the Heisenberg cell. The portrait is everywhere non-negative and holds the structure of the Wigner function in place. Wigner resolution carries no free parameter, set by the state's action capacity alone and sharpening as that capacity grows.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{Phase space formulation of quantum mechanics, Wigner functions, Uncertainty principle, Symplectic geometry, Schrödinger cat states}

% ===============================================================================
%  OPENING
% ===============================================================================
The Wigner function represents a quantum state in phase space~\cite{wigner1932,hillery1984}. It is defined pointwise below Planck's quantum of action, where Hudson's theorem forces it negative~\cite{hudson1974}. Its negativity is a resource for quantum advantage~\cite{veitch2012,marieisert2012} and a measure of nonclassicality~\cite{kenfack2004}. A non-negative distribution supports classical sampling~\cite{husimi1940,cartwright1976,degosson2005bullsci,dragoman2005phasespace,polkovnikov2010} and follows from smoothing the Wigner function at a freely chosen scale. Here the state's action capacity $\pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p$ sets that scale without a free parameter.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE PHASE-SPACE CELLS
% ===============================================================================
Planck divided phase space into elementary regions of action~\cite{planck1906}. Heisenberg's uncertainty principle $\sigma_x\,\sigma_p \ge \hbar/2$~\cite{kennard1927,robertson1929,schrodinger1930} fixes their minimum area. Geometrically, de Gosson gave this action region the form of the state's covariance ellipse, with symplectic capacity $\pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p$~\cite{degosson2009camel} and semi-axes $\Delta x = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_x$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_p$. This is the Heisenberg cell (Figs.~\ref{fig:principal_blobs},~\ref{fig:quantum_blobs}) of action capacity,
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2.
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}

% ===============================================================================
%  ZUREK SCALES AS A FAMILY OF DE GOSSON BLOBS
% ===============================================================================
The Wigner function carries structure at small scales. Zurek located those scales, $\delta p \sim \hbar/\Delta x$ and $\delta x \sim \hbar/\Delta p$, from the state's action capacity~\cite{zurek2001}. We identify them as the principal axes of a family of de Gosson quantum blobs (Fig.~\ref{fig:principal_blobs}),
\begin{equation}
a_{\theta=0} = \pi\,\Delta x\,\delta p = h/2, \qquad a_{\pi/2} = \pi\,\delta x\,\Delta p = h/2.
\label{eq:principal_axis_cells}
\end{equation}
The capacity $\Delta x, \Delta p$ runs along the major axes, the resolution $\delta x, \delta p$ along the minor.

Between these principal members the quantum blob family $\{a_\theta\}$ deforms across $\theta \in [0,\pi)$ at constant action $h/2$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:quantum_blobs}). Each blob $a_\theta$ is the preimage of the rigid ellipse $\hat a_\theta$ under the scaling $X = x/\Delta x$, $P = p/\Delta p$,
\begin{equation}
a_\theta = \left\{ (x, p) : (X, P) \in \hat a_\theta \right\}.
\label{eq:atheta}
\end{equation}
The rotated quadratures $X_\theta = X\cos\theta + P\sin\theta$ and $P_\theta = -X\sin\theta + P\cos\theta$ are the homodyne quadratures of optical tomography at phase $\theta$. In the scaled coordinates $\hat a_\theta$ is the rotation by $\theta$ of a single ellipse,
\begin{equation}
\hat a_\theta = \left\{ (X, P) : X_\theta^{2} + \tfrac{A^2}{(\pi\hbar)^2}\, P_\theta^{2} \le 1 \right\},
\label{eq:atheta_hat}
\end{equation}
inscribed in the unit disk at every $\theta$, so the Heisenberg cell $A$ circumscribes each blob $a_\theta$.

% ===============================================================================
%  PER ANGLE NON-NEGATIVITY: THE QUANTUM KERNEL
% ===============================================================================
The Wigner function of the state is
\begin{equation}
W(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(x+s)\,\psi(x-s)\,e^{2ips/\hbar}\,ds.
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
The Wigner function of each quantum blob $a_\theta$ is the centered Gaussian whose $h/2$ ellipse is $a_\theta$,
\begin{equation}
K_\theta(x, p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\exp\!\left[ -X_\theta^{2} - \tfrac{A^2}{(\pi\hbar)^2}\, P_\theta^{2} \right].
\label{eq:Ktheta}
\end{equation}
The convolution of $W$ with each quantum kernel $K_\theta$ is
\begin{equation}
\widetilde W_\theta(x,p) = (W * K_\theta)(x,p).
\label{eq:Wtheta}
\end{equation}
Convolving $W$ with the kernel $K_\theta$ of a single quantum blob $a_\theta$ lifts it up to Planck's quantum of action and yields a non-negative function~\cite{cartwright1976,degosson2005bullsci,cordero2018}.

%===============================================================================
%  ALL-ANGLES RESOLUTION: QUORUM CELL
%===============================================================================
Zurek also located the scale $a \sim \hbar \times (\hbar / A)$~\cite{zurek2001,zurek2003decoherence} of the chessboard structure in the compass state. We identify its rotationally invariant form as the quorum cell $\tilde a$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:cat}, column 1). Named after the quorum of observables in optical tomography~\cite{darianomacconeparis2001}, $\tilde a$ is the ellipse with semi-axes $\delta x, \delta p$ (Figs.~\ref{fig:principal_blobs},~\ref{fig:quantum_blobs}) and action
\begin{equation}
\tilde a = \pi\,\delta x\,\delta p = \frac{\pi\hbar^2}{\Delta x\,\Delta p}.
\label{eq:tildea}
\end{equation}
$\tilde a$ is the resolution of the state's finest structure in the bare
Wigner function (Figs.~\ref{fig:harmonic},~\ref{fig:eigen},~\ref{fig:cat},
column 1). The quorum cell $\tilde a$ is the symplectic polar dual of
the Heisenberg cell $A$~\cite{degosson2022polar,degosson2024reconstruction}.
The duality is reflexive, so $A$ and $\tilde a$ each fix the other, and
antimonotone, so $\tilde a$ shrinks as $A$ grows. Each blob $a_\theta$
is circumscribed by $A$ and circumscribes $\tilde a$, nesting as
$A \supseteq a_\theta \supseteq \tilde a$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:quantum_blobs}).
A quorum of these $a_\theta$ blobs, each respecting
$h/2$, reaches $\tilde a$ together. Resolution and capacity are reciprocal at the quantum of action,
$\tilde a\,A = (h/2)^2$.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE CONVOLVED PORTRAIT
% ===============================================================================
A single blob commits to a quadrature the state does not privilege. Integrating $\widetilde W_\theta$ over the family privileges none, giving the phase-space portrait of the state at the resolution $\tilde a$,
\begin{equation}
\widetilde{W}(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_0^\pi \widetilde W_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta.
\label{eq:tildeW}
\end{equation}
$\widetilde W_\theta \ge 0$ for every $\theta$, so $\widetilde W \ge 0$ everywhere. Where tomographic reconstruction inverts marginals pointwise to recover $W$~\cite{vogelrisken1989,lvovsky2009,degosson2022radon}, convolving $W$ with the family $\{K_\theta\}$ over the same $\theta$ builds the non-negative portrait $\widetilde W$.

% ===============================================================================
%  UNIQUENESS OF BLOBS TO STATE
% ===============================================================================
A quantum blob $a_\theta$ too large for $A$ to circumscribe, or too
small to circumscribe $\tilde a$, is a blob of another state and
portrays that state, not this one~\cite{degosson2013qblobs,
degosson2022polar}. Because every $h/2$ blob $a_\theta$ of this state circumscribes the
quorum cell $\tilde a$, and a portrait finer than $\tilde a$ everywhere
would smooth $W$ below the $h/2$ action non-negativity
requires~\cite{cartwright1976,degosson2005bullsci}, $\tilde a$ is the
finest resolution any non-negative portrait attains, and the family
$\{a_\theta\}$ reaches it across $\theta \in [0,\pi)$.

%===============================================================================
%  DIFFERENTIATION
%===============================================================================
A fixed vacuum-width Gaussian~\cite{husimi1940} smooths every state by the same amount, matching only the vacuum $A = h/2$ and erasing the fine structure of larger states $A > h/2$. Methods with a free parameter~\cite{wodkiewicz1984,appleby1998husimi,cahillglauber1969} resolve more finely, the scale chosen rather than derived. The scale here is derived. The state's action capacity $A$ fixes its quorum cell $\tilde a$ as the symplectic polar dual, and the family $\{a_\theta\}$ reaches $\tilde a$ with no quantity left free.

%===============================================================================
%  GENERALIZATION
%===============================================================================
The principal frame, where the Heisenberg cell $A$ is axis-aligned, costs no generality. The Wigner function transforms covariantly under the symplectic group~\cite{cordero2018}, so the map that diagonalizes a state's covariance carries it to this frame at preserved action. The Kerr crescent, a sheared coherent state, is resolved this way (Fig.~\ref{fig:eigen}). In one degree of freedom, $A$ fixes the family uniquely, and the sub-Planck structure is already present for states with $A > h/2$. In higher dimensions, action $h/2$ no longer singles out a blob~\cite{degosson2013qblobs}, symplectic capacity and volume diverge, and the higher-dimensional case remains for future work.

%===============================================================================
%  NUMERICAL RESULTS
%===============================================================================
Figures~\ref{fig:harmonic},~\ref{fig:eigen}, and~\ref{fig:cat} show numerical results across twelve states. The portrait is non-negative to machine precision, and the cross-spectral phase between $W$ and $\widetilde W$ vanishes, confirming that the smoothing displaces no feature~\cite{zenododeposit}.

% ===============================================================================
%  DISCUSSION
% ===============================================================================
The uncertainty product $\sigma_x \sigma_p$ grows unbounded, the
micro-to-macro transition Heisenberg~\cite{heisenberg1927} and
Bohr~\cite{bohr1928} identified as requiring special care. The quantum
blob does not. As $A$ grows, the family $\{a_\theta\}$ deforms at fixed action $h/2$. Squeezing reaches the same resolution by external apparatus~\cite{walls1983}, while here the deformation is set by the state. A Schr\"odinger cat with capacity $\Delta x \sim 10$ cm and $\Delta p \sim 1$ kg$\cdot$m/s carries structure in its Wigner function at $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p \sim 10^{-34}$ m~\cite{schrodinger1935trimmer}. The correspondence principle follows from $A \to \infty$, the physical limit underlying the heuristic $h \to 0$.

The scale called sub-Planck~\cite{zurek2001} is the minor axis $\delta$ of an $h/2$ blob, narrowing as the conjugate capacity $\Delta$ grows. The negative Wigner function $W$ and its non-negative portrait $\widetilde W$ have their structure at a single resolution $\tilde{a}$, set by the state's action capacity $A$. Heisenberg's product gives the capacity. The Zurek scales are the axes of de Gosson quantum blobs, and give the resolution. As the action capacity grows to $\pi \, \Delta x \, \Delta p \ge h/2$ the resolution sharpens to $\pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p) \le h/2$. We read this structure as the geometry of phase space itself, which de Gosson takes as prior to the uncertainty principle~\cite{degosson2026indeterminacy}.
