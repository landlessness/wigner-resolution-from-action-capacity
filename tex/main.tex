\title{Wigner Resolution from Action Capacity}

\author{Brian S. Mulloy\orcidlink{0000-0002-1803-3172}}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher, Corktown, Detroit, Michigan, USA}
\email{bmulloy@umich.edu}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
Phase-space formulations of quantum mechanics usually ignore that resolution is bounded below by Planck's quantum of action. In the Wigner formulation, the justification is that even though pointwise resolution forces the quasi-probability negative, those values carry physical structure. Zurek located that structure at scales set by the state's action capacity. We identify those scales as exactly the axes of de Gosson quantum blobs. Inscribed within the state's elliptical Heisenberg cell of action capacity $\pi \Delta x \Delta p$, these blobs form a family bitangent to the cell, deforming at constant action $h/2$. Convolving the Wigner function with a single $h/2$ blob restores resolution to Planck's quantum of action. Integrating over the family reaches $\pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$, the resolution symplectic polar duality sets on any non-negative portrait of the state, sharpening as the action capacity grows.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{Phase space formulation of quantum mechanics, Wigner functions, Uncertainty principle, Symplectic geometry, Schrödinger cat states}

% ===============================================================================
%  OPENING
% ===============================================================================
The Wigner function represents a quantum state in phase space~\cite{wigner1932,hillery1984}. It is defined pointwise, below Planck's quantum of action, and Hudson's theorem forces it negative there~\cite{hudson1974}. Its negativity is a resource for quantum advantage~\cite{veitch2012,marieisert2012} and has been characterized by quasi-probability measures~\cite{kenfack2004}. A non-negative portrait admits a probability interpretation in phase space~\cite{husimi1940,cartwright1976,dragoman2005phasespace,degosson2005bullsci} and supports classical sampling of quantum dynamics~\cite{polkovnikov2010}. In this letter, action capacity $\pi \, \Delta x\, \Delta p$ deforms a family of $h/2$ quantum blobs to resolve the Wigner function to $\pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$, the resolution that symplectic polar duality sets on any non-negative portrait of the state.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE PHASE-SPACE CELLS
% ===============================================================================
The state is constrained by the uncertainty principle $\sigma_x\,\sigma_p \ge \hbar/2$~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930}, where $\sigma_x, \sigma_p$ are the standard deviations of position and momentum. The state's covariance ellipsoid, with semi-axes $\Delta x = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_x$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_p$, has a symplectic capacity~\cite{degosson2009camel}, $\pi\Delta x\,\Delta p$, the action capacity of the state. The ellipsoid is its Heisenberg cell (Figs.~\ref{fig:zurek_blobs},~\ref{fig:bitangent_blobs}),
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2.
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}

% ===============================================================================
%  ZUREK SCALES AS A FAMILY OF DE GOSSON BLOBS
% ===============================================================================
Wigner negativity carries structure at small scales. Zurek located those scales at $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p$ and $\delta p = \hbar/\Delta x$, set by the state's action capacity~\cite{zurek2001}. We identify them as the principal axes of a family of de Gosson quantum blobs (Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_blobs}),
\begin{equation}
a_{\theta=0} = \pi\,\Delta x\,\delta p = h/2, \qquad a_{\pi/2} = \pi\,\delta x\,\Delta p = h/2.
\label{eq:principal_axis_cells}
\end{equation}
Zurek's relations give all four semi-axes exactly. The capacities $\Delta x, \Delta p$ run along the major axes, the resolutions $\delta x, \delta p$ along the minor.

Between these principal members the family deforms across $\theta \in [0,\pi)$. In coordinates $X = x/\Delta x$ and $P = p/\Delta p$, where the Heisenberg cell is the unit disk, the blob is
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
a_\theta = \Bigg\{ (X, P) : \;\;
& \left(X\cos\theta + P\sin\theta\right)^{2} \\
& {} + \tfrac{A^2}{(\pi\hbar)^2}\!\left(-X\sin\theta + P\cos\theta\right)^{2} \le 1 \Bigg\}.
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:atheta}
\end{equation}
The two squared terms are the homodyne quadratures of optical tomography at phase $\theta$. In one degree of freedom the symplectic capacity of an ellipse equals its area, so each $a_\theta$ is a quantum blob and the action is $h/2$ across every $\theta$. The axis ratio is $A/(\pi\hbar)$, equal to one at minimum capacity and growing with $A$.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE KERNEL
% ===============================================================================

The Wigner function of the state is
\begin{equation}
W(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(x+s)\,\psi(x-s)\,e^{2ips/\hbar}\,ds.
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
The Wigner function of the blob $a_\theta$ is the centered Gaussian whose covariance ellipse is $a_\theta$,
\begin{multline}
K_\theta(X, P) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\exp\bigg[-\!\left(X\cos\theta + P\sin\theta\right)^{2} \\
- \tfrac{A^2}{(\pi\hbar)^2}\!\left(-X\sin\theta + P\cos\theta\right)^{2} \bigg],
\label{eq:Ktheta}
\end{multline}
of action $h/2$ by construction. The convolution of $W$ with $K_\theta$ is
\begin{equation}
P_\theta(x,p) = (W * K_\theta)(x,p).
\label{eq:Ptheta}
\end{equation}
Convolving with a Gaussian of action $\ge h/2$ lifts $W$ up to Planck's quantum of action and yields a non-negative function~\cite{cartwright1976,degosson2005bullsci,cordero2018}, so $P_\theta$ is non-negative everywhere.

%===============================================================================
%  QUORUM CELL
%===============================================================================
Zurek also located the scale $a_Z \sim \hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$ of the chessboard structure in the compass state (Fig.~\ref{fig:cat}, column 1)~\cite{zurek2001}. Its rotationally invariant form is the quorum cell $\tilde a$. Named after the quorum of observables in optical tomography~\cite{darianomacconeparis2001}, $\tilde a$ is the ellipse with semi-axes $\delta x, \delta p$ and action
\begin{equation}
\tilde a = \pi\,\delta x\,\delta p = \frac{\pi\hbar^2}{\Delta x\,\Delta p}.
\label{eq:tildea}
\end{equation}
$\tilde a$ is the scale of the state's finest structure in the bare Wigner function (Figs.~\ref{fig:harmonic},~\ref{fig:eigen},~\ref{fig:cat}, column 1). No single measurement resolves it without going below $h/2$. A quorum of $h/2$ blobs reaches it together, each respecting the quantum of action. Resolution and capacity are reciprocal at the quantum of action, $\delta x\,\Delta p = \delta p\,\Delta x = \hbar$ and $\tilde a\,A = (h/2)^2$. The quorum cell is the symplectic polar dual of the Heisenberg cell $A$~\cite{degosson2022polar,degosson2024reconstruction}, and each blob $a_\theta$ is inscribed in $A$ and, as a cell of action $h/2$, circumscribes $\tilde a$: $\tilde a \subseteq a_\theta \subseteq A$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:bitangent_blobs}).

% ===============================================================================
%  THE CONVOLVED PORTRAIT
% ===============================================================================
Integrating $P_\theta$ over the family gives the phase-space portrait of the state at the resolution $\tilde a$,
\begin{equation}
\widetilde{W}(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_0^\pi P_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta.
\label{eq:tildeW}
\end{equation}
$P_\theta \ge 0$ for every $\theta$, and a non-negative integrand integrates to a non-negative function, so $\widetilde W \ge 0$ everywhere. Tomographic reconstruction inverts marginals to recover $W$ pointwise~\cite{vogelrisken1989,lvovsky2009,degosson2022radon}. The construction here instead convolves $W$ with $K_\theta$ and integrates over $\theta$.

The smallest kernel that yields a non-negative portrait is a quantum blob~\cite{cartwright1976,degosson2005bullsci,degosson2013qblobs}. A blob squeezed narrower than $\tilde a$, or one wider than $A$, is the cell of another state and portrays that state, not this one. Because every blob of the state contains the quorum cell, $\tilde a$ is the finest resolution any non-negative portrait attains, and the family reaches it across $\theta \in [0,\pi)$.

The construction is stated in the principal frame of the state, where the Heisenberg cell is aligned with the phase-space axes. This costs no generality. The Wigner function transforms covariantly under the symplectic group~\cite{cordero2018}, so any state is brought to this frame by the symplectic map that diagonalizes its covariance, and the family is carried back by the inverse. The Kerr crescent, a sheared coherent state, is resolved this way (Fig.~\ref{fig:eigen}): the family transforms with the cell, and the action of each blob is preserved. In one degree of freedom this family and the resolution it sets are rigid; in higher dimensions action $h/2$ no longer singles out a blob~\cite{degosson2013qblobs}, symplectic capacity and volume diverge, and the construction remains for future work.

%===============================================================================
%  DIFFERENTIATION
%===============================================================================
A fixed vacuum-width Gaussian~\cite{husimi1940} matches the state only at the vacuum $A \sim h/2$ and erases the structure at $\tilde a$ for any larger state. Methods with a free parameter~\cite{wodkiewicz1984,appleby1998husimi,cahillglauber1969} set the scale by hand, and whether any choice reaches $\tilde a$ is open. The family here carries no free parameter, fixed by the action capacity $A$ alone.

%===============================================================================
Figures~\ref{fig:harmonic},~\ref{fig:eigen}, and~\ref{fig:cat} show numerical results across twelve states, using scales $x_0 = \sqrt{\hbar/m\omega}$ and $p_0 = \sqrt{\hbar m\omega}$. Column 1 places $\tilde a$ on the bare Wigner function; column 5 shows the portrait $\widetilde W$ resolved to that scale, everywhere non-negative, with the positive lobes of $W$ reappearing in the same count and location~\cite{zenododeposit}.

% ===============================================================================
%  DISCUSSION
% ===============================================================================
The uncertainty product $\sigma_x \sigma_p$ grows unbounded, the
micro-to-macro transition Heisenberg~\cite{heisenberg1927} and
Bohr~\cite{bohr1928} identified as requiring special care. The quantum
blob does not. As $A$ grows, the action of $a_\theta$ is fixed at $h/2$.
Squeezing~\cite{walls1983} reaches the same reciprocity at
$\sigma_x\sigma_p = \hbar/2$ by external apparatus. Here it is inherent to the state. A Schr\"odinger cat with $\Delta x \sim 10$ cm and $\Delta p \sim 1$ kg$\cdot$m/s has positional resolution $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p \sim 10^{-34}$ m, twenty-four orders of magnitude below the size of the single radioactive atom that triggers its apparatus~\cite{schrodinger1935trimmer}. The correspondence principle follows from $A \to \infty$, the physical limit underlying the heuristic $h \to 0$. The scale called sub-Planck is the minor axis $\delta$ of an $h/2$ blob.

The structure of the negative Wigner function $W$ and its non-negative portrait $\widetilde W$ occur at the same scale, set by the action capacity of the state. Heisenberg's product gives the capacity. Zurek's scales and de Gosson's quantum blobs give the resolution. As the action capacity grows to $\pi \Delta x \Delta p \ge h/2$ the resolution sharpens to $\pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p) \le h/2$, a certainty complement to the uncertainty principle.
