\title{Wigner Resolution from Action Capacity}

\author{Brian S. Mulloy\orcidlink{0000-0002-1803-3172}}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher, Corktown, Detroit, Michigan, USA}
\email{bmulloy@umich.edu}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
Phase-space formulations of quantum mechanics usually ignore that resolution is bounded below by Planck's quantum of action. In the Wigner formulation, the justification is that even though pointwise resolution forces the quasi-probability negative, those negative values carry physical structure. Zurek located that structure at scales set by the state's action capacity. We identify those scales as exactly the axes of de Gosson quantum blobs. Inscribed within the state's elliptical Heisenberg cell of action capacity $\pi \Delta x \Delta p$, these blobs form a family bitangent to the cell, deforming at constant action $h/2$. Convolving the Wigner function with this $h/2$ family and integrating over the family lifts the resolution to Planck's floor and yields a non-negative portrait of resolution $\pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$, sharpening with capacity.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{Phase space formulation of quantum mechanics, Wigner functions, Uncertainty principle, Symplectic geometry, Schrödinger cat states}

% ===============================================================================
%  OPENING

The Wigner function represents a quantum state in phase space~\cite{wigner1932,hillery1984}. It is defined pointwise, below Planck's quantum of action, and Hudson's theorem forces it negative there~\cite{hudson1974}. Its negativity is a resource for quantum advantage~\cite{veitch2012,marieisert2012} and has been characterized by several quasi-probability measures~\cite{kenfack2004}. A non-negative portrait admits a literal probability interpretation in phase space~\cite{husimi1940,cartwright1976,dragoman2005phasespace,degosson2005bullsci} and supports classical sampling of quantum dynamics~\cite{polkovnikov2010}. This letter develops the non-negative portrait at the resolution permitted by the state's action capacity.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE PHASE-SPACE CELLS
% ===============================================================================
The state is constrained by the uncertainty principle $\sigma_x\,\sigma_p \ge \hbar/2$~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930}, where $\sigma_x, \sigma_p$ are the standard deviations of position and momentum. The state's covariance ellipsoid, with semi-axes $\Delta x = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_x$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_p$, has a symplectic capacity~\cite{degosson2009camel}, $\pi\Delta x\,\Delta p$, the action capacity of the state. The ellipsoid is its Heisenberg cell (Figs.~\ref{fig:zurek_blobs},~\ref{fig:bitangent_blobs}),
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2.
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}

% ===============================================================================
%  ZUREK SCALES AS A FAMILY OF DE GOSSON BLOBS
% ===============================================================================
Pointwise resolution sits below Planck's floor and forces the Wigner function negative~\cite{hudson1974}. The negativity carries structure at small scales. Zurek located those scales at $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p$ and $\delta p = \hbar/\Delta x$, set by the state's action capacity~\cite{zurek2001}. We identify them as the principal axes of a family of de Gosson quantum blobs (Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_blobs}),
\begin{equation}
a_{\theta=0} = \pi\,\Delta x\,\delta p = h/2, \qquad a_{\pi/2} = \pi\,\delta x\,\Delta p = h/2.
\label{eq:principal_axis_cells}
\end{equation}
Zurek's relations give all four semi-axes exactly. The capacities $\Delta x, \Delta p$ run along the major axes, the resolutions $\delta x, \delta p$ along the minor.

Zurek also located the scale $a_Z \sim \hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$ of the chessboard structure in the compass state (Fig.~\ref{fig:cat}, column 1)~\cite{zurek2001}. Its rotationally invariant form is the quorum cell $\tilde a$. Named after the quorum of observables in optical tomography~\cite{darianomacconeparis2001}, $\tilde a$ is the ellipse with semi-axes $\delta x, \delta p$ and action
\begin{equation}
\tilde a = \pi\,\delta x\,\delta p = \frac{\pi\hbar^2}{\Delta x\,\Delta p}.
\label{eq:tildea}
\end{equation}
The quorum cell is not a region the state occupies. It is the resolution a quorum of $h/2$ blobs reaches together, each respecting the floor. Resolution and capacity are reciprocal at the quantum of action. They obey $\delta\,\Delta = \hbar$ and $\tilde a \, A = (h/2)^2$.

The principal blobs are members of a family that deforms across $\theta$.
The quorum cell is the symplectic polar dual of the Heisenberg cell~\cite{degosson2022polar,degosson2024reconstruction}. Each blob $a_\theta$, $\theta \in [0,\pi)$, is bitangent to $A$ from within and to
$\tilde a$ from without, $\tilde a \subseteq a_\theta \subseteq A$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:bitangent_blobs}),
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
a_\theta = \Bigg\{ (X, P) : \;\;
& \left(X\cos\theta + P\sin\theta\right)^{2} \\
& {} + \tfrac{A^2}{(\pi\hbar)^2}\!\left(-X\sin\theta + P\cos\theta\right)^{2} \le 1 \Bigg\}.
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:atheta}
\end{equation}
The two squared terms are the homodyne quadratures of optical tomography at phase $\theta$. The action is $h/2$ across every $\theta$. The axis ratio is $A/(\pi\hbar)$, equal to one at minimum capacity and growing with $A$.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE BOUNDARY AND THE KERNEL
% ===============================================================================

The Wigner function of the state is
\begin{equation}
W(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(x+s)\,\psi(x-s)\,e^{2ips/\hbar}\,ds.
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
The Wigner function of the blob $a_\theta$ is the centered Gaussian whose covariance ellipse is $a_\theta$,
\begin{multline}
K_\theta(X, P) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\exp\bigg[-\!\left(X\cos\theta + P\sin\theta\right)^{2} \\
- \tfrac{A^2}{(\pi\hbar)^2}\!\left(-X\sin\theta + P\cos\theta\right)^{2} \bigg],
\label{eq:Ktheta}
\end{multline}
of action $h/2$ by construction. The convolution of $W$ with $K_\theta$ is
\begin{equation}
P_\theta(x,p) = (W * K_\theta)(x,p).
\label{eq:Ptheta}
\end{equation}
Convolving with a Gaussian of action $\ge h/2$ lifts the resolution to Planck's floor and yields a non-negative function~\cite{cartwright1976,degosson2005bullsci,cordero2018}, so $P_\theta$ is non-negative everywhere.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE CONVOLVED PORTRAIT
% ===============================================================================
Integrating $P_\theta$ over the family gives the phase-space portrait of the state at the resolution $\tilde a$,
\begin{equation}
\widetilde{W}(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_0^\pi P_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta.
\label{eq:tildeW}
\end{equation}
$P_\theta \ge 0$ for every $\theta$, and a non-negative integrand integrates to a non-negative function, so $\widetilde W \ge 0$ everywhere. Tomographic reconstruction inverts marginals to recover $W$ pointwise~\cite{vogelrisken1989,lvovsky2009,degosson2022radon}. The construction here convolves $W$ with $K_\theta$ and integrates over $\theta$.

The construction extends to rotated ellipsoids through the symplectic covariance of the family~\cite{cordero2018}, and to higher dimensions through the symplectic capacity bounded by the Robertson--Schr\"odinger inequality~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930,degosson2013qblobs},
\begin{equation}
\sigma_x^2 \sigma_p^2 - \mathrm{Cov}(x,p)^2 \ge \left(\frac{\hbar}{2}\right)^2.
\label{eq:rs}
\end{equation}

Other non-negative portraits~\cite{husimi1940,hudson1974,cartwright1976,polkovnikov2010,cahillglauber1969} reach scales set by $\Delta$ or $\delta$, coincident at $A \sim h/2$. Whether blob deformation underlies them beyond $A \sim h/2$ is open.

%===============================================================================
%  NUMERICAL RESULTS
%===============================================================================
Figures~\ref{fig:eigen} and~\ref{fig:cat} show numerical results across eight states. $\widetilde W$ is non-negative across all eight. The positive lobes of $W$ in column 1 reappear in $\widetilde W$ in column 5, in the same count and location, resolved at the scale of the quorum cell $\tilde a$. Every member of the family is a de Gosson quantum blob of action $h/2$, verified numerically across $\theta$ and across the eight states~\cite{zenododeposit}.

% ===============================================================================
%  DISCUSSION
% ===============================================================================
The uncertainty product $\sigma_x \sigma_p$ grows unbounded, the
micro-to-macro transition Heisenberg~\cite{heisenberg1927} and
Bohr~\cite{bohr1928} identified as requiring special care. The bitangent
blob does not. As $A$ grows, the action of $a_\theta$ is fixed at $h/2$,
the resolution $\delta = \hbar/\Delta$ sharpening with capacity.
Squeezing~\cite{walls1983} reaches the same reciprocity at
$\sigma_x\sigma_p = \hbar/2$ by external apparatus. Here it is inherent to the state. A Schr\"odinger cat with $\Delta x \sim 10$ cm and $\Delta p \sim 1$ kg$\cdot$m/s has positional resolution $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p \sim 10^{-34}$ m, twenty-four orders of magnitude below the size of the single radioactive atom that triggers its apparatus~\cite{schrodinger1935trimmer}. The correspondence principle follows from $A \to \infty$, the physical limit underlying the heuristic $h \to 0$. The scales called sub-Planck are Planckian by construction, bounded at $h/2$ with resolution set by the state's action capacity.
