\title{Wigner Resolution from Action Capacity}

\author{Brian S. Mulloy\orcidlink{0000-0002-1803-3172}}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher, Corktown, Detroit, Michigan, USA}
\email{bmulloy@umich.edu}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
Phase-space formulations of quantum mechanics usually ignore that resolution is bounded below by Planck's quantum of action. In the Wigner formulation, the justification is that even though pointwise resolution forces the quasi-probability negative, those negative values carry physical structure. Zurek located that structure at scales set by the state's action capacity. We identify those scales as exactly the axes of de Gosson quantum blobs. Inscribed within the state's elliptical Heisenberg cell of action capacity $\pi \Delta x \Delta p$, these blobs form a family bitangent to the cell, deforming at constant action $h/2$. Convolving the Wigner function with this $h/2$ family and integrating over the family yields a non-negative portrait at resolution $\pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$, the floor that symplectic polar duality sets on any non-negative portrait of the state.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{Phase space formulation of quantum mechanics, Wigner functions, Uncertainty principle, Symplectic geometry, Schrödinger cat states}

% ===============================================================================
%  OPENING
% ===============================================================================
The Wigner function represents a quantum state in phase space~\cite{wigner1932,hillery1984}. It is defined pointwise, below Planck's quantum of action, and Hudson's theorem forces it negative there~\cite{hudson1974}. Its negativity is a resource for quantum advantage~\cite{veitch2012,marieisert2012} and has been characterized by several quasi-probability measures~\cite{kenfack2004}. A non-negative portrait admits a literal probability interpretation in phase space~\cite{husimi1940,cartwright1976,dragoman2005phasespace,degosson2005bullsci} and supports classical sampling of quantum dynamics~\cite{polkovnikov2010}. This letter resolves the Wigner function at the floor set by the state's action capacity, the finest resolution a non-negative portrait admits.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE PHASE-SPACE CELLS
% ===============================================================================
The state is constrained by the uncertainty principle $\sigma_x\,\sigma_p \ge \hbar/2$~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930}, where $\sigma_x, \sigma_p$ are the standard deviations of position and momentum. The state's covariance ellipsoid, with semi-axes $\Delta x = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_x$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_p$, has a symplectic capacity~\cite{degosson2009camel}, $\pi\Delta x\,\Delta p$, the action capacity of the state. The ellipsoid is its Heisenberg cell (Figs.~\ref{fig:zurek_blobs},~\ref{fig:bitangent_blobs}),
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2.
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}

% ===============================================================================
%  ZUREK SCALES AS A FAMILY OF DE GOSSON BLOBS
% ===============================================================================
Wigner negativity carries structure at small scales. Zurek located those scales at $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p$ and $\delta p = \hbar/\Delta x$, set by the state's action capacity~\cite{zurek2001}. We identify them as the principal axes of a family of de Gosson quantum blobs (Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_blobs}),
\begin{equation}
a_{\theta=0} = \pi\,\Delta x\,\delta p = h/2, \qquad a_{\pi/2} = \pi\,\delta x\,\Delta p = h/2.
\label{eq:principal_axis_cells}
\end{equation}
Zurek's relations give all four semi-axes exactly. The capacities $\Delta x, \Delta p$ run along the major axes, the resolutions $\delta x, \delta p$ along the minor.

Zurek also located the scale $a_Z \sim \hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$ of the chessboard structure in the compass state (Fig.~\ref{fig:cat}, column 1)~\cite{zurek2001}. Its rotationally invariant form is the quorum cell $\tilde a$. Named after the quorum of observables in optical tomography~\cite{darianomacconeparis2001}, $\tilde a$ is the ellipse with semi-axes $\delta x, \delta p$ and action
\begin{equation}
\tilde a = \pi\,\delta x\,\delta p = \frac{\pi\hbar^2}{\Delta x\,\Delta p}.
\label{eq:tildea}
\end{equation}
The quorum cell $\tilde a$ is the scale of the state's finest structure, the interference detail Zurek located, present in the Wigner function before any construction (Figs.~\ref{fig:harmonic},~\ref{fig:eigen},~\ref{fig:cat}, column 1). No single measurement resolves it without crossing the floor. A quorum of $h/2$ blobs reaches it together, each respecting the floor. Resolution and capacity are reciprocal at the quantum of action, $\delta x\,\Delta p = \delta p\,\Delta x = \hbar$ and $\tilde a\,A = (h/2)^2$.

The principal blobs are members of a family that deforms across $\theta$.
The quorum cell $\tilde a$ is the symplectic polar dual of the Heisenberg cell $A$~\cite{degosson2022polar,degosson2024reconstruction}. Each blob $a_\theta$, $\theta \in [0,\pi)$, is inscribed in $A$. As a cell of action $h/2$ it circumscribes $\tilde a$ through the reciprocal product $\tilde a\,A = (h/2)^2$, $\tilde a \subseteq a_\theta \subseteq A$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:bitangent_blobs}),
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
a_\theta = \Bigg\{ (X, P) : \;\;
& \left(X\cos\theta + P\sin\theta\right)^{2} \\
& {} + \tfrac{A^2}{(\pi\hbar)^2}\!\left(-X\sin\theta + P\cos\theta\right)^{2} \le 1 \Bigg\}.
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:atheta}
\end{equation}
The two squared terms are the homodyne quadratures of optical tomography at phase $\theta$. In one degree of freedom the symplectic capacity of an ellipse equals its area, so each $a_\theta$ is a quantum blob and the action is $h/2$ across every $\theta$. The axis ratio is $A/(\pi\hbar)$, equal to one at minimum capacity and growing with $A$.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE BOUNDARY AND THE KERNEL
% ===============================================================================

The Wigner function of the state is
\begin{equation}
W(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(x+s)\,\psi(x-s)\,e^{2ips/\hbar}\,ds.
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
The Wigner function of the blob $a_\theta$ is the centered Gaussian whose covariance ellipse is $a_\theta$,
\begin{multline}
K_\theta(X, P) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\exp\bigg[-\!\left(X\cos\theta + P\sin\theta\right)^{2} \\
- \tfrac{A^2}{(\pi\hbar)^2}\!\left(-X\sin\theta + P\cos\theta\right)^{2} \bigg],
\label{eq:Ktheta}
\end{multline}
of action $h/2$ by construction. The convolution of $W$ with $K_\theta$ is
\begin{equation}
P_\theta(x,p) = (W * K_\theta)(x,p).
\label{eq:Ptheta}
\end{equation}
Convolving with a Gaussian of action $\ge h/2$ lifts the resolution to Planck's floor and yields a non-negative function~\cite{cartwright1976,degosson2005bullsci,cordero2018}, so $P_\theta$ is non-negative everywhere.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE CONVOLVED PORTRAIT
% ===============================================================================
Integrating $P_\theta$ over the family gives the phase-space portrait of the state at the resolution $\tilde a$,
\begin{equation}
\widetilde{W}(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_0^\pi P_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta.
\label{eq:tildeW}
\end{equation}
$P_\theta \ge 0$ for every $\theta$, and a non-negative integrand integrates to a non-negative function, so $\widetilde W \ge 0$ everywhere. Tomographic reconstruction inverts marginals to recover $W$ pointwise~\cite{vogelrisken1989,lvovsky2009,degosson2022radon}. The construction here convolves $W$ with $K_\theta$ and integrates over $\theta$.

The resolution $\tilde a$ is the floor. A non-negative portrait of the state convolves $W$ with a kernel of action at least $h/2$~\cite{cartwright1976,degosson2005bullsci}, and at action $h/2$ that kernel is a quantum blob, the covariance of a pure Gaussian state~\cite{degosson2013qblobs}. A blob of the state is inscribed in its Heisenberg cell, $\tilde a \subseteq a_\theta \subseteq A$~\cite{degosson2022polar,degosson2024reconstruction}. A blob squeezed past $\tilde a$, or one wider than $A$, is the cell of another state and portrays that state, not this one.

The lower inclusion is the floor. Every blob of the state contains the quorum cell, $\tilde a \subseteq a_\theta$, so no non-negative portrait resolves finer than $\tilde a$. The upper inclusion attains it. At the principal quadratures $a_\theta$ meets $A$ along its major axis and $\tilde a$ along its minor axis. As $\theta$ turns between them the contact rolls off the axes, while $a_\theta$ stays inscribed in $A$ and circumscribed about $\tilde a$ throughout. Across $\theta \in [0,\pi)$ the family covers every quadrature, so it resolves the state to $\tilde a$. Symplectic polar duality is anti-monotone and reflexive~\cite{degosson2022polar,degosson2024reconstruction}, and carries the reciprocal product $\tilde a\,A = (h/2)^2$, so the dual scale is fixed by the Heisenberg cell, $\tilde a = \pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$. The portrait $\widetilde W$ reaches it.

The construction extends to rotated ellipsoids through the symplectic covariance of the family~\cite{cordero2018}. In one degree of freedom every centered ellipse of action $h/2$ is a quantum blob, so the inscribed family and the floor it sets are rigid. In higher dimensions action $h/2$ no longer singles out a blob, and inscription leaves the symplectic spectrum underdetermined~\cite{degosson2013qblobs}. Symplectic capacity and volume diverge there, with the action capacity bounded by the Robertson--Schr\"odinger inequality~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930}. The construction there remains for future work.

Other non-negative portraits carry a free parameter, covariance-matched generalized Husimi smoothing~\cite{husimi1940,cartwright1976} and the Cahill--Glauber $s$-ordered family~\cite{cahillglauber1969}, reaching scales near the state's capacity or resolution, coincident at $A \sim h/2$. Whether their free parameter, selected to match the state, saturates the floor $\tilde a$ is an open question.

%===============================================================================
%  NUMERICAL RESULTS
%===============================================================================
Figures~\ref{fig:harmonic},~\ref{fig:eigen}, and~\ref{fig:cat} show numerical results across twelve states. In column 1 the quorum cell $\tilde a$ overlies the finest structure of the bare Wigner function, the interference detail at the scale Zurek located, before any convolution. Convolving with the family then resolves the state to that same scale: $\widetilde W$ (column 5) is non-negative across all twelve, resolved at the floor $\tilde a$, with the positive lobes of $W$ reappearing in the same count and location. Every member of the family is a de Gosson quantum blob of action $h/2$, verified numerically across $\theta$ and across the twelve states~\cite{zenododeposit}.

% ===============================================================================
%  DISCUSSION
% ===============================================================================
The uncertainty product $\sigma_x \sigma_p$ grows unbounded, the
micro-to-macro transition Heisenberg~\cite{heisenberg1927} and
Bohr~\cite{bohr1928} identified as requiring special care. The quantum
blob does not. As $A$ grows, the action of $a_\theta$ is fixed at $h/2$.
Squeezing~\cite{walls1983} reaches the same reciprocity at
$\sigma_x\sigma_p = \hbar/2$ by external apparatus. Here it is inherent to the state. A Schr\"odinger cat with $\Delta x \sim 10$ cm and $\Delta p \sim 1$ kg$\cdot$m/s has positional resolution $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p \sim 10^{-34}$ m, twenty-four orders of magnitude below the size of the single radioactive atom that triggers its apparatus~\cite{schrodinger1935trimmer}. The correspondence principle follows from $A \to \infty$, the physical limit underlying the heuristic $h \to 0$. The scale called sub-Planck is the minor axis $\delta$ of an $h/2$ blob.

The structure of the negative Wigner function $W$ and its non-negative portrait $\widetilde W$ occur at the same scale, $\pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$, set by the action capacity of the state. As the state grows, its capacity $\Delta$ widens and its resolution $\delta$ sharpens, reciprocal at the quantum of action $h$. The uncertainty principle is the capacity. Zurek's scales and de Gosson's quantum blobs are the resolution, the complement to the Heisenberg uncertainty principle.
