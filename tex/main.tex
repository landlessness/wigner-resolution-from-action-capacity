\title{Wigner Resolution from Action Capacity}

\author{Brian S. Mulloy\orcidlink{0000-0002-1803-3172}}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher, Corktown, Detroit, Michigan, USA}
\email{bmulloy@umich.edu}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
Phase-space formulations of quantum mechanics usually ignore that resolution is bounded below by Planck's quantum of action. In the Wigner formulation, the justification is that even though pointwise resolution forces the quasi-probability negative, those values carry physical structure. Zurek located that structure at scales set by the state's action capacity. We identify those scales as the principal axes of de Gosson quantum blobs. The state's elliptical Heisenberg cell of action capacity $\pi \, \Delta x \, \Delta p$ circumscribes these blobs, a family deforming at constant action $h/2$. Convolving the Wigner function to the scale of a single $h/2$ blob restores resolution to Planck's quantum of action and renders it non-negative. Integrating over the family reaches $\pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$, the resolution symplectic polar duality sets on any non-negative portrait of the state, sharpening as the action capacity grows.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{Phase space formulation of quantum mechanics, Wigner functions, Uncertainty principle, Symplectic geometry, Schrödinger cat states}

% ===============================================================================
%  OPENING
% ===============================================================================
The Wigner function represents a quantum state in phase space~\cite{wigner1932,hillery1984}. It is defined pointwise, below Planck's quantum of action, and Hudson's theorem forces it negative there~\cite{hudson1974}. Its negativity is a resource for quantum advantage~\cite{veitch2012,marieisert2012} and has been characterized by quasi-probability measures~\cite{kenfack2004}. A non-negative portrait admits a probability interpretation in phase space~\cite{husimi1940,cartwright1976,dragoman2005phasespace,degosson2005bullsci} and supports classical sampling of quantum dynamics~\cite{polkovnikov2010}. In this letter, action capacity $\pi \, \Delta x\, \Delta p$ deforms a family of $h/2$ quantum blobs to resolve the Wigner function to $\pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$, the resolution that symplectic polar duality sets on any non-negative portrait of the state.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE PHASE-SPACE CELLS
% ===============================================================================
Planck divided phase space into elementary regions of action~\cite{planck1906}. Heisenberg's uncertainty principle $\sigma_x\,\sigma_p \ge \hbar/2$~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930} fixes the limit. Geometrically, de Gosson gave this action region the form of the state's covariance ellipsoid, with symplectic capacity $\pi \, \Delta x\,\Delta p$~\cite{degosson2009camel} and semi-axes $\Delta x = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_x$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_p$. This is the Heisenberg cell of action capacity (Figs.~\ref{fig:principal_blobs},~\ref{fig:quantum_blobs}),
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2.
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}

% ===============================================================================
%  ZUREK SCALES AS A FAMILY OF DE GOSSON BLOBS
% ===============================================================================
The Wigner function carries structure at small scales. Zurek located those scales at $\delta x \simeq \hbar/\sigma_p$ and $\delta p \simeq \hbar/\sigma_x$, set by the state's action capacity~\cite{zurek2001}. We identify them as the principal axes of a family of de Gosson quantum blobs (Fig.~\ref{fig:principal_blobs}),
\begin{equation}
a_{\theta=0} = \pi\,\Delta x\,\delta p = h/2, \qquad a_{\pi/2} = \pi\,\delta x\,\Delta p = h/2.
\label{eq:principal_axis_cells}
\end{equation}
Zurek's relations set the scale of all four semi-axes. The capacity $\Delta x, \Delta p$ runs along the major axes, the resolution $\delta x, \delta p$ along the minor.

Between these principal members the quantum blob $a_\theta$ deforms across $\theta \in [0,\pi)$ at constant action $h/2$, the image of a single rigid ellipse $\hat a_\theta$ under the scaling $X = x/\Delta x$, $P = p/\Delta p$,
\begin{equation}
a_\theta = \left\{ (x, p) : (X, P) \in \hat a_\theta \right\}.
\label{eq:atheta}
\end{equation}
The rotated quadratures $X_\theta = X\cos\theta + P\sin\theta$ and $P_\theta = -X\sin\theta + P\cos\theta$ are the homodyne quadratures of optical tomography at phase $\theta$. In the scaled coordinates $\hat a_\theta$ is the rotation by $\theta$ of a single ellipse,
\begin{equation}
\hat a_\theta = \left\{ (X, P) : X_\theta^{2} + \tfrac{A^2}{(\pi\hbar)^2}\, P_\theta^{2} \le 1 \right\}.
\label{eq:atheta_hat}
\end{equation}
The Heisenberg cell $A$ circumscribes the blob $a_\theta$.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE QUANTUM KERNEL
% ===============================================================================
The Wigner function of the state is
\begin{equation}
W(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(x+s)\,\psi(x-s)\,e^{2ips/\hbar}\,ds.
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
The Wigner function of the quantum blob $a_\theta$ is the centered Gaussian whose $h/2$ ellipse is $a_\theta$,
\begin{equation}
K_\theta(x, p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\exp\!\left[ -X_\theta^{2} - \tfrac{A^2}{(\pi\hbar)^2}\, P_\theta^{2} \right].
\label{eq:Ktheta}
\end{equation} The convolution of $W$ with the quantum kernel $K_\theta$ is
\begin{equation}
\widetilde W_\theta(x,p) = (W * K_\theta)(x,p).
\label{eq:Wtheta}
\end{equation}
Convolving with a Gaussian of action $h/2$ lifts $W$ up to Planck's quantum of action and yields a non-negative function~\cite{cartwright1976,degosson2005bullsci,cordero2018}, so $\widetilde W_\theta$ is non-negative everywhere.

%===============================================================================
%  QUORUM CELL
%===============================================================================
Zurek also located the scale $a_Z \sim \hbar^2/(\sigma_x\,\sigma_p)$ of the chessboard structure in the compass state (Fig.~\ref{fig:cat}, column 1)~\cite{zurek2001}. Its rotationally invariant form is the quorum cell $\tilde a$. Named after the quorum of observables in optical tomography~\cite{darianomacconeparis2001}, $\tilde a$ is the ellipse with semi-axes $\delta x, \delta p$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:principal_blobs}) and action
\begin{equation}
\tilde a = \pi\,\delta x\,\delta p = \frac{\pi\hbar^2}{\Delta x\,\Delta p}.
\label{eq:tildea}
\end{equation}
$\tilde a$ is the scale of the state's finest structure in the bare Wigner function (Figs.~\ref{fig:harmonic},~\ref{fig:eigen},~\ref{fig:cat}, column 1). The quorum cell is the symplectic polar dual of the Heisenberg cell $A$~\cite{degosson2022polar,degosson2024reconstruction}. Each blob $a_\theta$ is circumscribed by $A$ and circumscribes $\tilde a$, nesting as $A \supseteq a_\theta \supseteq \tilde a$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:quantum_blobs}). A quorum of these blobs reaches $\tilde a$ together, each respecting $h/2$. Resolution and capacity are reciprocal at the quantum of action, $\tilde a\,A = (h/2)^2$.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE CONVOLVED PORTRAIT
% ===============================================================================
Integrating $\widetilde W_\theta$ over the family gives the phase-space portrait of the state at the resolution $\tilde a$,
\begin{equation}
\widetilde{W}(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_0^\pi \widetilde W_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta.
\label{eq:tildeW}
\end{equation}
$\widetilde W_\theta \ge 0$ for every $\theta$, so $\widetilde W \ge 0$ everywhere. Tomographic reconstruction inverts marginals to recover $W$ pointwise~\cite{vogelrisken1989,lvovsky2009,degosson2022radon}. Over the same $\theta$, the construction here convolves $W$ to build the non-negative portrait $\widetilde W$.

% ===============================================================================
%  UNIQUENESS OF BLOBS TO STATE
% ===============================================================================
A quantum blob $a_\theta$ too large for $A$ to circumscribe, or too small to circumscribe $\tilde a$, is a blob of another state and portrays that state, not this one. Because every blob of this state circumscribes the quorum cell, $\tilde a$ is the finest resolution any non-negative portrait attains, and the family reaches it across $\theta \in [0,\pi)$.

%===============================================================================
%  GENERALIZATION
%===============================================================================
The construction is stated in the principal frame, where the Heisenberg cell is axis-aligned. This costs no generality. The Wigner function transforms covariantly under the symplectic group~\cite{cordero2018}, so the map that diagonalizes a state's covariance carries it to this frame at preserved action. The Kerr crescent, a sheared coherent state, is resolved this way (Fig.~\ref{fig:eigen}). In one degree of freedom, $A$ fixes the family uniquely. In higher dimensions, action $h/2$ no longer singles out a blob~\cite{degosson2013qblobs}, symplectic capacity and volume diverge, and the construction remains for future work.

%===============================================================================
%  DIFFERENTIATION
%===============================================================================
A fixed vacuum-width Gaussian~\cite{husimi1940} matches the state only at the vacuum $A \sim h/2$ and erases structure at $\tilde a$ for any larger state. Methods with a free parameter~\cite{wodkiewicz1984,appleby1998husimi,cahillglauber1969} can resolve more finely, the scale chosen as a parameter. The family here carries no free parameter, fixed by the action capacity $A$ alone.

%  NUMERICAL RESULTS
%===============================================================================
Figures~\ref{fig:harmonic},~\ref{fig:eigen}, and~\ref{fig:cat} show numerical results across twelve states~\cite{zenododeposit}, using scales $x_0 = \sqrt{\hbar/m\omega}$ and $p_0 = \sqrt{\hbar m\omega}$. Column 1 places $\tilde a$ on the bare Wigner function. Column 5 shows the portrait $\widetilde W$ resolved to that scale. It is non-negative everywhere, with the positive lobes of $W$ reappearing in the same count and location.

% ===============================================================================
%  DISCUSSION
% ===============================================================================
The uncertainty product $\sigma_x \sigma_p$ grows unbounded, the
micro-to-macro transition Heisenberg~\cite{heisenberg1927} and
Bohr~\cite{bohr1928} identified as requiring special care. The quantum
blob does not. As $A$ grows, the action of $a_\theta$ is fixed at $h/2$.
Squeezing~\cite{walls1983} reaches $\sigma_x\sigma_p = \hbar/2$ by external apparatus. Here it is inherent to the state. A Schr\"odinger cat with $\Delta x \sim 10$ cm and $\Delta p \sim 1$ kg$\cdot$m/s has positional resolution $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p \sim 10^{-34}$ m, vastly smaller than the single radioactive atom that triggers its apparatus~\cite{schrodinger1935trimmer}. The correspondence principle follows from $A \to \infty$, the physical limit underlying the heuristic $h \to 0$. The scale called sub-Planck is the minor axis $\delta$ of an $h/2$ blob. We read this structure as the geometry of phase space itself, which de Gosson takes as prior to the uncertainty principle~\cite{degosson2026indeterminacy}.

The negative Wigner function $W$ and its non-negative portrait $\widetilde W$ have their structure at a single scale, set by the action capacity of the state. Heisenberg's product gives the capacity. Zurek's scales and de Gosson's quantum blobs give the resolution. As the action capacity grows to $\pi \, \Delta x \, \Delta p \ge h/2$ the resolution sharpens to $\pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p) \le h/2$.
